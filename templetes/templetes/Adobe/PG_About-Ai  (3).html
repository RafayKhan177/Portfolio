Does Adobe Illustrator CC feature effects and graphics?

Compared to AutoCAD and PaintTool SAI, Adobe Illustrator comes with various vector drawing tools, which can help you create scalable graphics. The term ‘resolution independent’ perfectly defines ‘vectors’. It means you can resize several elements in the creation without compromising on quality. With Illustrator CC, this property extends to the tool’s ‘effects’ section. In spite of the modified resolution, shadows, blurs, and textures, your creations are able to retain quality. Additionally, the program provides support for cross-media design. As such, the layout can adapt to different preferences, whether you edit or draw something for a mobile phone, online publishing platforms, or digital printing. Adobe Illustrator features a rich brush palette. In fact, you also get the opportunity to create new brush styles. The ‘Bristle’ and ‘Blob’ brush are two good examples. While the former gives a natural look with realistic strokes, the latter can generate a single vector shape. Some other prominent features worth mentioning include 3D effects, live distortion, the possibility to blend shapes, wide array of symbols, shape builder, Gaussian blur, and more.

Does Adobe Illustrator CC have an intuitive interface?

Unlike competitors, Illustrator features an intuitive interface. Therefore, the program is easy to navigate and allows you to maximize productivity. While working in this tool, you can move around fluidly to manage different workspaces. Moreover, you can easily maintain various layout changes or reset parameters. The program provides you with a wide range of essential tools, which can ensure precise drawings and sketches with vector graphics. Illustrator is powered by a solid image-tracing engine, which ensures your creations are accurate and clean. While applying gradients on strokes or objects, you get complete control over placement and opacity. Without a doubt, Illustrator lets you perform various functions with ease.

Fortunately, it only takes a few attempts before you get a hang of the various features and tools. It’s worth mentioning that Adobe Illustrator comes with a set of preset templates. There’s also a ‘Help’ section, which can answer your queries and doubts. If you want to try a few basic features in the program, you’ll find Illustrator quite easy to use. With the step-by-step tutorials, you can cover the basics as well as advanced techniques. Everything has been organized in a crystal clear manner. The company keeps making improvements, such as customizable toolbars, global editing, freeform gradients, trim/presentation previews, and more.
